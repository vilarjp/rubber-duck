# PRD-To-Plan Alignment

When a plan is created from a PRD, the plan must prove that the approved product intent survived translation into implementation work.

Check alignment before asking for plan approval:

- Every PRD goal has at least one corresponding planned implementation or verification item.
- Every acceptance criterion is mapped to a planned test, check, manual verification step, or explicit non-implementation rationale.
- Non-goals remain out of scope.
- User-facing behavior, API/UI behavior, and success signals are preserved or intentionally narrowed with rationale.
- PRD risks, dependencies, assumptions, and blocking-question answers are carried into technical risks, sequencing, tests, or rollout notes when they affect implementation.
- Any technical decision that changes product scope is recorded as a blocking question or explicit human-approved decision.

Use this as a stricter alignment check inside the existing plan workflow. Do not create a separate revision document unless the human asks for one.
